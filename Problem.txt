TECHNICAL REPORT: ADJUSTMENT OF BRAIN NETWORK ANALYSIS METHODOLOGY

Original Topic: Graph Analysis of DTI Images in Alzheimer's Disease

Revised Topic: Structural Brain Network Analysis of MRI Images in Alzheimer's Disease

1. Academic Rationale for Methodological Adjustment
Limited Availability of Clinical DTI Data
Diffusion Tensor Imaging (DTI) data from Alzheimer's disease patients are difficult to access due to strict medical data privacy regulations.
The Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI) dataset requires ethical approval, institutional verification, and a rigorous application process, which typically takes between three and six months.
OASIS-3, which contains diffusion MRI data, requires a separate research account verification process and extended data access approval.
During the project period, only the publicly accessible OASIS-1 and OASIS-2 datasets were readily available.
Characteristics of OASIS-1 and OASIS-2
OASIS-1 and OASIS-2 primarily provide T1-weighted structural MRI (MPRAGE) scans.
These datasets do not include diffusion imaging information such as b-values and b-vectors required for DTI/DWI processing.
Consequently, direct white matter tractography and diffusion-based connectivity analysis cannot be performed using these datasets.
Alternative Scientific Approach: Structural Covariance Network (SCN)
To maintain project feasibility while preserving the scientific foundation of graph-theoretical brain network analysis, the study was redirected toward Structural Covariance Network (SCN) analysis using T1-weighted MRI data from OASIS-1 and OASIS-2.
SCN constructs brain graphs based on the linear covariance of gray matter morphology across brain regions within a population.
In this study, 64 Regions of Interest (ROIs) were extracted, and inter-regional covariance was used to construct structural brain networks.
Structural covariance analysis is a widely accepted and extensively used methodology in Alzheimer's disease neuroimaging research.
2. Project Deliverables

The project consists of two complementary components designed to demonstrate both technical competence and clinical applicability.

A. DTI Methodological Proof of Concept

A complete DTI processing framework was implemented, covering the entire workflow from preprocessing to graph analysis:

Task 2 – Denoising and Gibbs Artifact Removal
Diffusion MRI denoising using the Patch2Self algorithm (Ridge Regression-based self-supervised denoising).
Gibbs ringing artifact correction.
Task 3 – Tractography
Implementation of Tensor and Constant Solid Angle (CSA) diffusion models.
Deterministic tractography for white matter fiber reconstruction.
Construction of structural connectivity matrices based on fiber count and fractional anisotropy (FA).
Task 4 – Graph Feature Extraction

Extraction of five core graph-theoretical metrics:

Degree
Clustering Coefficient
Characteristic Path Length
Global Efficiency
Small-Worldness
Task 5 – Statistical Analysis
Regression analysis investigating the relationship between network topology and disease severity.
Experimental Dataset
Stanford HARDI dataset, automatically downloaded through the processing pipeline.
Significance

This component demonstrates full technical capability in DTI processing, tractography reconstruction, and graph-based connectome analysis.

B. Clinical Alzheimer's Disease Cohort Analysis

Two structural covariance network pipelines were successfully established and evaluated on real Alzheimer's disease cohorts.

OASIS-1 Pipeline

File: scripts/run_oasis.py

38 subjects analyzed.
23 subjects with available clinical CDR and MMSE assessments.
Disc 7 subset.
OASIS-2 Pipeline

File: scripts/run_oasis2.py

209 longitudinal sessions.
82 unique subjects.
Multi-year follow-up cohort.
Optimization Techniques
Stride-based MRI downsampling for approximately 10× faster tissue segmentation (CSF, GM, WM).
Regional feature caching using regional_gm.npy.
Subsequent network analyses and visualization generation reduced to approximately one second.
Scientific Findings
The normalized OASIS-2 Structural Covariance Network achieved a Small-Worldness index of 1.8982, consistent with the small-world topology observed in healthy brain networks.
Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression demonstrated that normalized Whole Brain Volume (nWBV) is strongly associated with:
Cognitive performance measured by MMSE (p < 0.001)
Dementia severity measured by CDR (p < 0.001)
